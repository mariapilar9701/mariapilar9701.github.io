Intersectionality

The analytical framework for understanding the complexities that make up our world within individual unique experiences. Intersectionality includes all aspect of individual identities such as (but not limited to): race, gender, sexuality, social class, political background, physical/mental disabilities, and more.

"In essence, however, intersectionality is 'a way of thinking about identity and its relationship to power.'"
Kimberlé Crenshaw, 2015

Why is intersectionality important?

Understanding intersectionality and the diverse backgrounds that create individuals allows us to recognize inequalities and the aspects of our culture that need meaningful change. This could be in our educations, work environments.

Implicit Bias

Implicit bias is an unconcious negative prejudice or attitude against a specific group. This could be in association with race, gender, and more.

Implicit Bias Test

The Implicit Bias Tests from Harvard measure the automatic associations between concepts with evaluations or stereotypes.

The options for tests include topics:

  • Race
  • Transgender/Cisgender
  • Jewish People/Judaism
  • Gender-Career
  • ..and more!

Additional Resources

Joy Buolamwini TED Talk – How I’m fighting bias in algorithms

Joy Buolamwini is a black, female computer scientist whose career shifted focus upon her graduate studies at MIT. She had faced issues throughout her academic and professional career related to racially biased facial recognition software. This sort of algorithmic bias dates to the first camera, and how lighting settings were adjusted for white, or fair-skinned people. Now, this bias has caused issues in so many aspects of our lives such as criminal justice and hiring practices. The more artificial intelligence software is used across industries, the larger the magnitude of what Joy calls the “coded gaze” is. Joy has started the Algorithmic Justice League a movement toward “equitable and accountable AI” with innumerous efforts to better these systems we so heavily rely on.

A research-developed toolkit you can install on your devices to measure and lessen the bias within the algorithms you use. It available in Python or R code and uses over seventy different measures of individual and group fairness.
